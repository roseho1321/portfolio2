Hi, I'm Rose – a multidisplinary designer based in Washington DC. I am passionate about storytelling, accessible and inclusive design, and creating cohesive experiences that drive impact.

Most recently, I was a UX designer at Foundation Medicine working alongside healthcare providers and biopharma partners to transform cancer care through genomic profiling.

Previous, I have worked on experiencial education at Education First, reimagining the volunteering experience at sustainabe development non-profit Casa Congo, and inspiring through storytelling at Global Expeditions Group.

My design process consists of understanding where the project falls in the greater system, empathizing with users, synthesizing compelling and strategic naratives, exploring a breadth of approaches, and tieing everything together with beautiful and cohesive prototypes.

I recently completed Designership’s Figma Masterclass and received a certificate from IDEO’s Human-Centered Design Strategy course. I received my Bachelor's of Science in Psychology and Human Computer Interaction from Washington University in St. Louis as a Danforth Scholar.

In my free time, you can catch me climbing, learning Spanish, and trying out new recipes. Currently, my interests include regenerative agriculture, food forests, and volunteering with Soul Trak to ensure that the outdoors is a space for everyone.
