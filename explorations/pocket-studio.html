Pocket Studio

Concept / Design / Prototype

2020

Pocket Studio is a mobile app dedicated to remotely establishing real-life dance experiences with VR. We explore how we can use technology to allow greater access to movement therapy and connect people through their passion.

Background

Dance has existed as a cultural practice, an art form, and a way of connection for as long as human society has existed. Not only is dancing a great way to reduce stress and anxiety, it is also comes with a variety of physical health benefits such as improving condition of heart and lungs, muscular strength, endurance and motor fitness, enhancing coordination, agility and flexibility, and improving balance and spatial awareness. Dance is also a powerful tool for social connection.

However, during COVID-19, or for individuals who are unable to attend traditional studio classes, they face a wide range of pain points. Physical pain points include lack of space and mirrors in their homes, difficulty following along to two-dimensional videos, and dealing with noise and other distractions. Learning dance virtually also loses the feeling of community and lacks the schedule and structure of a studio schedule.

Concept

Pocket Studio is a platform dedicated to remotely establish real-life dance experiences with VR technology, in order to bring the mental, physical, and social benefits of dance to a post-COVID world.

Participants can choose personalized classes based on the size of the space, their preferred dance styles, and the difficulty of the class.

Pocket Studio explores the future of dance. Classes are available to the user through onboarding. A search tab enables viewing other classes and genres. The open studio VR component allows unlimited practice time and a customized environment. Dance challenges help users test their dance skills with a competitive element. Users can take classes and practice with friends, send and receive messages, and compete on a team.

Research and Ideation

It’s been really difficult for everyone. Dance is a huge part of my life and I’ve been really missing it during the pandemic.

Maria Sanchez, Dancer

For the research portion of the project, we conducted a survey of over 50 dancers. After synthesizing the findings, we then sketched potential solutions. We conducted in depth interviews with 8 participants and used this feedback to choose the direction and create wireframes.
